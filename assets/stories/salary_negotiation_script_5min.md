# Salary Negotiation Script — 5 Minute Short-Form Video

---

**HOOK (Narrator/Text on screen):**
"Can you talk your way into a raise? John's about to try — with the toughest boss alive."

---

**JOHN:** Alice, got a minute? I pulled up our Q3 numbers and... we 20x'd our organic leads. Twenty. Times.

**ALICE:** Oh yeah, I saw that. Great numbers.

**JOHN:** Great numbers that deserve a great raise, don't you think?

**ALICE:** *(laughs)* Hold on. You said it yourself — "organic." That means people came to us on their own. Word of mouth. What exactly did you do... besides exist?

**JOHN:** Besides exist?! I built the referral framework. I wrote the campaign playbook. I made the content people actually wanted to share.

**ALICE:** So you're telling me you want a raise... for making people talk for free?

**JOHN:** I want a raise for making people WANT to talk for free. That's harder than paying for ads, Alice.

**ALICE:** *(leans back, arms crossed)* Convince me.

**JOHN:** Alright. Let's talk numbers. Before the referral program, we were spending fourteen thousand a month on paid ads. Cost per lead — forty-two dollars. My organic strategy? Cost per lead dropped to under three dollars. That's a 93% reduction in acquisition cost.

**ALICE:** *(nods slowly)* The numbers are solid. I'll give you that. But John, a strategy working doesn't automatically mean a raise. Lots of things work. The coffee machine works. I'm not giving it a bonus.

**JOHN:** *(slight laugh, but pushing through)* Okay but the coffee machine didn't generate eight hundred qualified leads in one quarter. I personally built the ambassador program, onboarded forty-two brand advocates, and created every piece of shareable content that drove those referrals.

**ALICE:** And I appreciate the hustle. I genuinely do. But you were hired to do marketing. You did marketing. That's... the job.

**JOHN:** *(beat, measured tone)* So exceeding targets by 2,000% is just... doing the job?

**ALICE:** Exceeding targets is what good employees do. That's why we hired you — because we expected you to be good.

**JOHN:** Alice, with all due respect, I benchmarked this against industry standards. The average organic growth rate in our sector is 15% quarter over quarter. We didn't do 15%. We did 1,900%. That doesn't just happen because someone shows up and does the job.

**ALICE:** *(straightens in her chair)* And with all due respect, John, I have a budget. I can't hand out raises every time someone has a good quarter. What happens next quarter if the numbers dip? Do I take the raise back?

**JOHN:** That's — no, that's not how compensation works. You reward proven performance. I've proven it. Three quarters in a row now, growth has been accelerating. This isn't a fluke.

**ALICE:** Three quarters is not a long time.

**JOHN:** It's three quarters more data than most people bring to this conversation.

**ALICE:** *(pause, voice calm but firm)* John. I hear you. But the answer right now is: the budget doesn't support it. It's not personal.

**JOHN:** *(exhales, trying to stay composed)* It feels personal when the results are sitting right there and the answer is still no.

**ALICE:** That's business. Results matter, but so does timing. And the timing isn't right.

**JOHN:** *(quieter, more deliberate)* Can I be honest with you?

**ALICE:** Always.

**JOHN:** I had two recruiters reach out to me last month. I didn't respond. You know why? Because I actually like working here. I believe in what we're building. But Alice... I need to feel like that loyalty is a two-way street.

**ALICE:** *(a flicker of concern, quickly composed)* I appreciate your loyalty. And I don't want you to feel undervalued. But I also can't make decisions based on outside offers. That's — that sets a bad precedent.

**JOHN:** I'm not leveraging offers. I'm telling you I chose to stay. And I'm asking you to make that choice feel worth it.

**ALICE:** *(long pause, tapping her pen)* John, if I gave every person on this team a raise because they turned down a recruiter, the department would be bankrupt by Friday.

**JOHN:** *(visibly stung, but holding it together)* I'm not every person on this team. And you know that.

**ALICE:** *(softer, but still firm)* I do know that. Which is exactly why I need you to trust me when I say — not right now. That doesn't mean not ever.

**JOHN:** *(nods slowly, clearly disappointed)* "Not right now" is what people say when they want you to stop asking.

**ALICE:** *(meets his eyes)* It's what I say when I mean not right now.

*(Silence. Both hold eye contact for a beat.)*

**JOHN:** *(stands, professional but deflated)* I respect your position, Alice. I do. But I want you to know — I remember this conversation. And next quarter, when the numbers come in again, I'm going to be sitting right back in this chair.

**ALICE:** *(slight smile, but tired)* I wouldn't expect anything less.

**JOHN:** *(turns to leave, pauses at the door)* For the record... I'm worth it.

**ALICE:** *(watches him go, exhales, mutters to herself)* ...Yeah. That's what worries me.
